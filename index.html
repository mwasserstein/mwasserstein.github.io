About

About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Atmospheric Sciences at the University of Utah, where I study orographic precipitation in Utah's Wasatch mountains. My research combines observational analysis, high-resolution numerical simulations, and data science techniques to understand the dynamics and predictability of mountain snowfall.

I earned my B.A. in Physics from Middlebury College in 2021, and my M.S. in Atmospheric Sciences from the University of Utah in 2023. My master's thesis characterized orographic snowfall extremes in the central Wasatch using long-term observational datasets.

My interest in weather began as a ski racer in Connecticut, where freeze-thaw cycles made every snowstorm feel like a gift. That early curiosity grew into a career focused on better understanding the complex physics that govern precipitation in mountainous terrain.
